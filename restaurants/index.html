The Four Revenue Leaks in Every Restaurant

No-Shows That Destroy Your Night

A party of 6 books for Saturday night. They never show. That is $270 in lost revenue, a table that sat empty during peak hours, and food that was prepped for nobody. Across a month, no-shows cost you thousands.

Estimated cost: $2,000 - $4,500/month

One-Time Visitors Who Disappear

A customer has a great meal. They leave happy. They never come back -- not because the food was bad, but because nothing reminded them you exist. Without a retention system, 60-70% of first-time diners never return.

Estimated cost: $1,500 - $3,000/month in lost repeat business

Competitors With More Reviews

The restaurant down the street has 280 Google reviews. You have 45. Their food is not better -- they just ask for reviews consistently. They rank higher in local search and get more walk-ins and reservations.

Estimated cost: $1,000 - $2,500/month in lost inbound traffic

Events and Promotions Nobody Sees

You spend hours planning a wine dinner or seasonal menu launch. You post on social media once. 3% of your followers see it. The event is half-empty. Your customer database -- the people who actually eat at your restaurant -- never got a direct message.

Estimated cost: $500 - $1,500/month in underperforming events

How We Fix It for Restaurants

1

Reservation Confirmation and Reminders

Every reservation triggers an immediate confirmation, a 24-hour text reminder, and a 2-hour heads-up. This alone reduces no-shows by 30-50%. The texts include a one-tap option to cancel or reschedule so you can fill the table instead of losing it.

2

No-Show Recovery

When a customer does not show, they get an automated message within an hour offering to rebook. No judgment -- just an easy path back. 25-40% of no-shows rebook. That is revenue recovered from what would have been $0.

3

Automated Review Collection

Two hours after the reservation time, every guest receives a text with a direct Google review link. Short, personal, takes 30 seconds. Restaurants that implement this see their review count grow 2-3x faster. More reviews means higher local search ranking means more reservations.

4

Repeat Customer Campaigns

30 days after a visit, customers get a personalized message -- a new menu highlight, a special offer, or an invitation to an upcoming event. 60 days, another touch. 90 days, a final re-engagement. This brings 15-20% of lapsed customers back for another visit.

5

Event and Promotion Automation

Launching a seasonal menu? Hosting a private event? Instead of posting once on social media and hoping, automated outreach goes directly to your customer database -- segmented by visit frequency and preferences. Fill events faster with the people most likely to show up.

6

Instant Inquiry Response

When someone asks about hours, availability, private dining, or catering through your website or social media, they get an immediate response. Your staff does not have to monitor messages around the clock. The customer gets answers and your team stays focused on service.
