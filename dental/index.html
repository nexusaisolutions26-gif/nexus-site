The Four Problems Draining Your Dental Practice

No-Shows Bleeding Your Schedule Dry

A patient skips their Thursday cleaning. The chair sits empty. Nobody follows up that day. By Friday, they have forgotten entirely. Multiply that across a month and entire days of production vanish from your schedule.

Estimated cost: $3,000 - $6,000/month

Recall Lists That Nobody Works

30-40% of your patients are overdue for hygiene, exams, or follow-up treatment. Your front desk means to reach out but is buried in daily tasks. Every month those patients drift further away -- and closer to a competitor down the road.

Estimated cost: $2,000 - $4,000/month in lost recurring revenue

Manual Intake Eating 30+ Minutes Per Patient

New patients arrive and spend half their appointment time filling out clipboards. Your front desk is manually entering data. The hygienist is waiting. The schedule backs up. Every new patient costs you time you cannot bill for.

Estimated cost: $800 - $1,500/month in overhead

Competitors Outranking You With More Reviews

The practice two miles away has 300 Google reviews. You have 47. Patients searching for a new dentist see their name first. Without a systematic review generation process, you lose new patients before they ever visit your website.

Estimated cost: $1,500 - $3,000/month in missed new patients

How We Fix It for Dental Practices

1

Appointment Confirmation Sequences

72-hour email confirmation. 24-hour text reminder with one-tap confirm or reschedule. 2-hour same-day reminder. This three-touch sequence reduces no-shows by 35-50% compared to a single reminder. 98% of texts are opened within 3 minutes -- your chair stays full and your schedule stays on track.

2

Automated Recall System

Patients due for cleanings, exams, or follow-up treatment get automated outreach at exactly the right interval. No more pulling recall reports and manually working the list. Practices that automate recall see 25-35% higher patient retention and fill hygiene chairs that would otherwise sit empty.

3

Digital Intake Automation

New patients receive intake forms, medical history questionnaires, and insurance information requests before they walk through the door. Check-in drops from 30 minutes to under 5. Your front desk greets patients instead of handing them clipboards, and the hygienist starts on time.

4

Review Generation Engine

Two hours after checkout, patients receive a direct link to leave a Google review. Sent while the experience is fresh and the cleaning still feels good. More reviews means higher local search rankings means more new patient inquiries. The cycle compounds every single month.

5

Reactivation Campaigns

Patients who have not visited in 6, 12, or 18 months receive targeted outreach sequences. No judgment, no pressure -- just a simple message that makes rebooking easy. Reactivation campaigns typically recover 15-25% of lapsed patients, turning a dormant database into active production.

6

Treatment Plan Follow-Up

When a patient leaves with an unscheduled treatment plan -- a crown, an implant, a deep cleaning -- they receive automated follow-up at 48 hours, one week, and two weeks. Case acceptance rates climb 20-30% when patients get timely reminders about treatment they already agreed they needed.
