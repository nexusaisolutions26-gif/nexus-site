The Five Revenue Leaks in Every Construction Company

Leads That Go Cold on the Jobsite

A homeowner submits a request for a kitchen remodel. You are on a jobsite 40 minutes away. By the time you respond that evening, they have already spoken to two other contractors. Speed-to-lead is everything -- and most construction firms lose this race daily.

Estimated cost: $3,000 - $8,000/month in lost projects

Estimates That Disappear Into Inboxes

You spend 90 minutes putting together a detailed estimate. You send it. The client goes quiet. You mean to follow up but another job takes priority. Three weeks later, they signed with someone else. Not because your price was wrong -- because you did not follow up.

Estimated cost: $2,500 - $6,000/month in unsigned estimates

Subcontractor Scheduling Chaos

The electrician thought he was coming Tuesday. The plumber was told Wednesday. Nobody confirmed. Now you have a day where two trades show up at the same time and another day where nobody shows up. Project timelines slip and margins shrink.

Estimated cost: $1,500 - $4,000/month in schedule delays

A Thin Review Profile That Loses Bids

You have completed 200 projects over the past 5 years. You have 12 Google reviews. The company with half your experience has 85 reviews and gets the inbound leads. Homeowners trust review counts -- and yours does not reflect your work quality.

Estimated cost: $1,500 - $4,000/month in lost inbound leads
