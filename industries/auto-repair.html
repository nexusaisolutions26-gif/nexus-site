The Problems Holding Your Shop Back

  • Customers only come in when something fails. Most vehicle owners know they should keep up with maintenance, but life gets in the way. Without reminders for oil changes, tire rotations, brake inspections, and fluid flushes, they wait until a warning light comes on -- and by then, they might end up at whatever shop is closest, not yours.
  • Online leads go cold before you respond. Someone searches for a mechanic, finds your Google listing, and submits a request for a quote or appointment. Your service writer is under a car. The shop phone is ringing. By the time they respond, the customer has already taken their vehicle to a shop that answered faster.
  • No-shows leave bays empty and technicians idle. A customer schedules a brake job for Tuesday morning. No confirmation or reminder goes out. They forget or decide to push it off. Your technician has an open bay. You lose the labor revenue and have no way to fill that slot on short notice.
  • Declined services never get followed up on. Your technician performs an inspection and recommends a timing belt replacement and new shocks. The customer declines -- not because they disagree, but because they are not ready today. Nobody follows up. The work either goes undone or gets done at another shop months later.
  • You are competing with chains that have marketing machines. National chains and quick lube franchises have corporate marketing teams running automated reminders, email campaigns, and review generation at scale. Independent shops deliver better work and better service, but without those same systems, they lose customers to convenience and visibility.

What Nexus AI Builds for Auto Repair Shops

Automated Service Reminders

Based on the services performed and manufacturer-recommended intervals, customers receive timely reminders when their vehicle is due for maintenance. Oil changes, tire rotations, brake inspections, fluid services -- each one triggers an automated message at the right time, bringing customers back before they even think to go elsewhere.

Instant Lead Response

Every online inquiry -- website form, Google message, social media DM -- receives an immediate, professional response. The system captures the vehicle information and service need, answers common questions, and moves the customer toward booking an appointment. No lead waits for your service writer to get off the lift.

Appointment Confirmations and Reminders

Every scheduled customer receives automated confirmations and reminders via text and email. They can confirm with a single tap. If they cancel, the system immediately reaches out to customers on your waitlist to fill the opening. No-shows become the exception, not the norm.

Declined Service Follow-Up

When a customer declines a recommended repair, the system records it and follows up at appropriate intervals. The messaging reinforces the importance of the service, addresses common objections, and makes it easy for the customer to schedule when they are ready. Work that used to be forgotten comes back.

Post-Service Review Requests

After every completed repair or service, the customer receives a request to share their experience. Happy customers are directed to Google. Negative feedback is captured privately, giving you the chance to resolve issues before they become public complaints. Your online reputation grows steadily and authentically.

Customer Reactivation Campaigns

Customers who have not visited in six months or more are automatically identified and receive a re-engagement sequence. The messaging is personalized to their vehicle and service history. Reactivating even a fraction of lapsed customers adds consistent monthly revenue without any advertising cost.
