The Cheapest Way to Get a Real AI Plan for Your Small Business in 2026

May 9, 2026 · 6 min read

If you're running a 1-10 person business and you've spent any time on the internet this year, you've been pitched roughly four versions of "let me help you with AI": a $5K/month consulting retainer, a $2K "AI strategy session," a $497 written assessment, and a free 30-minute discovery call that's actually a sales call.

None of those is the cheapest path. The cheapest path is one most consultants don't tell you about because it doesn't pay them well enough.

This is an honest breakdown of every real option, what each costs, what you actually get, and where the breakpoints are.

Option 1: Free — read three good blog posts and try one thing

Cost: $0. Time: ~2 hours.

If your business is genuinely 1 person and you've never installed an AI tool, the cheapest path is genuinely free. Read three sources, pick the single highest-leverage idea, install it, and see what happens for 30 days.

What you actually get: an opinion that may or may not fit your business, with no accountability if it doesn't work.

Best for: Solo operators who already feel like they know which area is the bottleneck and just need to be pointed at a specific tool.

What's missing: a written plan tied to your specific operations, install steps that account for your existing stack, and an honest "skip this" filter for the AI tools that get marketed hardest because they have the biggest budgets.

Option 2: $20/month — ChatGPT Plus and self-direction

Cost: $20/month. Time: ~10-20 hours of self-directed exploration.

Subscribe to ChatGPT Plus, ask it to audit your business, follow the recommendations.

This works better than people think for a certain kind of person — typically someone who already runs a tight operation, knows the specific bottleneck, and just wants help drafting a setup plan. ChatGPT will give you a plausible plan in about 30 seconds.

The trap: ChatGPT doesn't know your stack, doesn't know what's already broken, will recommend tools that don't exist anymore, and won't push back when your idea is wrong. It's a smart intern, not an experienced consultant.

This still beats hiring a $5K/month "AI consultant" for a sub-$5M business 90% of the time. Just understand its limits.
