1. The bottleneck we're solving

Northgate currently has 47 Google reviews, 4.6 stars. Customers visit roughly 60-80 times per week. Industry benchmark for an auto repair shop at this volume: 200-400 Google reviews. The shop is operating with 8-15% of the social proof a competitor with the same volume could have if they asked systematically.

The cost of low review count for an auto repair shop is mostly invisible — it shows up as the prospects who never call because a competitor with 312 reviews ranked higher in their search. Industry data: shops in the top quartile of Google review count for their zip get 40-65% more inbound new-customer calls than shops with similar service quality but fewer reviews.

At Northgate's average new-customer first-visit value of $480, even a 25% lift in new-customer call volume from improved Google ranking represents ~$5K-$8K/month of incremental new-customer revenue. Reviews are the single highest-leverage marketing input for an established local service business.

3. The review-request SMS template

Hi {{first_name}}, thanks for trusting Northgate with your {{vehicle_year_make_model}}. Quick favor — would you mind leaving us a Google review? Helps a ton. {{review_link}} Anything not 5 stars? Reply here first and we'll make it right before you post anything. — {{tech_first_name}} at Northgate

The structure matters. The "anything not 5 stars" line is the private-feedback escape hatch that catches unhappy customers BEFORE they post a 1-3 star review publicly. This is the single biggest reason this flow works versus generic "please leave us a review" texts.

4. Install steps for Birdeye

  1. Sign up at birdeye.com — request a 14-day trial first to test the Mitchell integration end-to-end
  2. Connect Mitchell shop-management software (Birdeye support handles this in onboarding — typically 30 min)
  3. Map the customer-data fields: first name, mobile phone, vehicle, technician, RO completion date
  4. Set the trigger: "2 hours after RO marked complete + customer paid"
  5. Paste the SMS template above (Birdeye supports the personalization tokens shown)
  6. Configure the rating filter: 4-5 stars routes to public Google review request, 1-3 stars routes to internal-only feedback form
  7. Add the shop's Google Business Profile review link to the template variable
  8. Run the trial for 14 days. Verify: every completed RO produces an SMS, the SMS arrives within 2 hours of pickup, the rating filter routes correctly

6. The 30-day measurement plan

  1. SMS send rate. Target: 90%+ of completed ROs produce an SMS within 2 hours. If <75%, the Mitchell integration has a data-quality issue (missing phone numbers, etc).
  2. SMS → click-through rate. Target: 25%+. If <15%, the template feels too generic — try the alternate one in the alternate-path section.
  3. Click-through → review-posted rate. Target: 40%+. If <25%, the Google review process is friction-loading — verify the link goes directly to the review page, not to GBP listing.
  4. Net new public reviews/week. Target: 5+. Floor for "this is working."
  5. Star rating drift. Target: stable at 4.6+ or rising. The 1-3 star private routing should keep public ratings from dropping despite higher review volume.
Stop-and-pivot threshold: If after 30 days you're getting fewer than 3 net new reviews/week, the issue is almost always the personalization tokens — customers can tell when a "Hi {{first_name}}" message has the literal text instead of their name. Check Birdeye's field-mapping setup and re-test.
