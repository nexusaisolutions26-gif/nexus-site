3. Install steps for Tier 0 (auto-text on missed call)

  1. Log into RingCentral admin portal at service.ringcentral.com
  2. Navigate to Phone System → Auto-Receptionist → General Settings → After-Hours Settings
  3. Enable Missed Call Behavior → Send Text Reply
  4. Paste this template into the message body:
    "Thanks for calling Hartwell HVAC — sorry we missed you. Reply here with your address and what's going on, and we'll text you back within 60 minutes during business hours. Emergency? Reply URGENT and we'll call you back."
  5. Set business hours: Mon-Sat 7 AM - 7 PM ET
  6. Set after-hours auto-reply (slightly different): "Thanks for calling — closed for the night. Reply with your address and what's going on, we'll text you first thing tomorrow at 7 AM. URGENT for true emergencies."
  7. Save and verify by calling the office line from a test phone outside business hours

5. The 30-day measurement plan

Track these metrics weekly. Threshold for "this is working" is highlighted.

  1. Auto-text replies received per week. Target: 4+. If <2, the message template needs rewriting (likely too generic).
  2. Conversion from auto-text reply to booked job. Target: 50%+. If <30%, the team's response cadence is the bottleneck, not the tool.
  3. Recovered revenue/month attributed to the tool. Target: $1,500+. Tag every customer that came in via auto-text in your CRM with a "recovered-missed-call" tag for clean reporting.
  4. Voicemail count. Target: trending down to near-zero. If still >5/week, the auto-text is not actually firing — re-verify configuration.
Stop-and-pivot threshold: If after 30 days you've recovered less than $400/month, the issue is not the tool. The most common cause is that the team isn't responding to auto-text replies fast enough. In that case, escalate to Tier 1 (Goodcall AI) so the AI handles the response itself.
