Part 1: The math

What an after-hours lead is actually worth

For a typical service business: a missed lead during business hours has a 50-70% recovery rate (callbacks work). A missed after-hours lead has a 5-8% recovery rate without intervention. The gap is where this system pays back.

Voicemail decay curve

Time after missed callLead recovery probability
0-60 seconds (auto-text fires)~60%
2-15 minutes (manual callback)~40%
1-2 hours (next-morning text)~25%
Next morning callback~15%
Voicemail-only, never followed up~5%

ROI worksheet for your business

  1. Pull your last 30 days of phone logs. Count missed calls outside business hours.
  2. Multiply by your typical close rate (e.g., 25% for residential service).
  3. Multiply by your average customer value (first year revenue).
  4. Multiply by 0.55 (the recovery uplift from this system).

Example: 20 after-hours misses/month × 25% close × $800 avg ticket × 55% uplift = $2,200/month recovered.

If your worksheet number is below $300/month, this system is overkill. Stop here and just use the free auto-text setting. If it's above $1,000/month, this system is your highest-ROI move this quarter.

Part 2: Free-tier setup (the foundation)

Every modern phone system has auto-text-on-missed-call as a built-in free feature. Setup details for 12 systems:

Google Voice

voice.google.com → Settings → Calls → "Send text when you miss a call" → toggle on, paste your message, save.

OpenPhone

App → Settings → Auto-replies → Add "Missed call" → Pick numbers → Paste message → Set schedule (24/7 or after-hours-only).

RingCentral

Web app → Settings → Phone → Auto receptionist → After-hours rules → Find "Missed call SMS" → enable + paste message.

Vonage / 8x8 / Dialpad / Nextiva / Ooma / Phone.com / Sideline / Grasshopper

Look for any of: "Auto-reply on missed call", "Missed call text", "After-hours rules → Send SMS". If your provider doesn't have it, forward your business number to a free Google Voice line.

The 8 message templates that work

  1. Service business standard: "Hi, this is [name] at [biz]. Missed your call — text me what you need and I'll get back tomorrow morning. Reply 'urgent' if it can't wait."
  2. After-hours specific: "Hey, [biz] is closed for the night — got your call though. Text me what you need and I'll respond by 9 AM. If it's an emergency reply 'urgent'."
  3. B2B / professional: "Hi, [name] from [biz] — I missed your call. Reply with what you need and I'll get back same business day. Or schedule directly: [your-cal-link]."
  4. Healthcare: "Hi! [biz] here. We missed your call. Text us your name + reason and we'll respond within 4 business hours. Existing patient? Reply 'patient'."
  5. High-volume retail: "Hi, [biz]. Missed your call — text 'STORE' for hours, 'ORDER' to check on an order, or anything else for a callback in 1 business day."
  6. Solo operator: "Hey, [name] here. Missed your call — heads down on a job. Text me what you need. If it's an emergency reply 'now'."
  7. Multi-location: "Hi, you reached [biz] [city]. Text us what you need + your zip code so we can route you to the closest team."
  8. New customer focused: "Hi! Got your call. New customer? Text 'NEW' and we'll send our most popular service options. Existing customer? Just text us what you need."

The 60-second response test

  1. Call your business number from your personal cell.
  2. Don't pick up.
  3. Wait 60-90 seconds.
  4. Verify the auto-text on your personal cell.

Trial-period evaluation framework

Most have free trials. Evaluate on:

  1. Voice quality: would your customers think it's a person? Listen to a recorded test call.
  2. Capture rate: what % of trial calls produced structured data in your CRM?
  3. Booking conversion: if it offers callback scheduling, what % accepted?
  4. Edge cases: Spanish callers, hard-to-understand callers, complex multi-step questions — does it gracefully escalate or fail awkwardly?

Red flags during trials

  • The AI invents service offerings you don't actually have.
  • Customers comment that the AI "sounds robotic" within the first 30 seconds.
  • CRM data quality is worse than what you'd get from manual transcription.
  • Service charges extra for features you assumed were standard (CRM integration, after-hours coverage, etc.).

Voice quality test methodology

  1. Have 3 different people (different accents, different tech-comfort levels) call the AI as test customers.
  2. Score each call: did the AI capture all needed info? Did the customer sound annoyed? Did they hang up early?
  3. Reject any service where 1+ of 3 testers had a negative experience.

Part 5: CRM integration

Auto-tagging missed-call-recovered leads

Every recovered lead gets recovered-from-missed-call in your CRM. Setup:

  • Zapier: Trigger "New SMS from VoIP" → Filter for auto-reply → "Find or Create Contact" → "Add Tag".
  • Make.com: Webhook from VoIP → Filter recent contact-text → CRM contact action → Tag action.
  • HubSpot Free: Email-to-contact via forwarded auto-reply → manual tag for first 30 days → automate via workflow.

Tracking close rate vs answered-live calls

After 30 days, run two filtered reports:

  • Deals from contacts with recovered-from-missed-call tag → close rate %
  • Deals from contacts WITHOUT the tag → close rate %

Recovered leads typically close at 60-80% of live-answer rate. If yours are below 50%, your auto-text wording is probably too generic.

Pipeline templates for after-hours leads

Create a separate pipeline stage: "After-hours - awaiting morning callback". Move recovered leads here automatically. Daily morning huddle reviews the queue.

Part 6: 30-day rollout playbook

Week 1: Free tier setup

  • Day 1: Enable auto-text on your phone system. Use Template 1 or 2 from Part 2.
  • Day 2: Run the 60-second response test. Verify it fires.
  • Day 3: Set up CRM tagging via Zapier or Make. Test with one missed call.
  • Day 4-7: Monitor real missed calls. Note any wording issues with your template.

Week 2: Refine + measure

  • Adjust wording if reply rate is below 30%.
  • Add CRM-side workflow: when contact is auto-tagged, create a follow-up task for next morning.
  • Track total missed calls vs total recovered leads.

Week 3: Decide on AI receptionist upgrade

  • Calculate your missed-call volume × close rate × ticket size from Week 1-2 data.
  • If above $1,000/month potential, sign up for one $40/month AI receptionist trial.
  • Run side-by-side comparison: 50% of after-hours calls go to free auto-text, 50% go to AI.

Week 4: Audit + commit

  • Review month 1 data: total recovered leads, total recovered revenue, ROI.
  • If AI trial outperformed free by $1,000+/mo: keep it.
  • If not: cancel the trial, stay on free tier.
  • Document the setup so a team member could replicate it elsewhere.
