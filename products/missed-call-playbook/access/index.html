The Missed-Call Text-Back Playbook

A step-by-step setup guide for turning every missed call into a recovered lead.

Why this matters

A missed call in a service business is a missed lead. A missed lead worth $200-2,000 (depending on your average ticket) costs you $200-2,000.

The fix is the simplest revenue-recovery setting in modern phone systems: auto-text on missed call. Recipient gets a text within 60 seconds. They reply with what they need. You respond next morning. Lead saved.

Recovery rates by response time

  • Texted within 60 seconds: ~60% recovery
  • Returned next morning by phone: ~25% recovery
  • Voicemail-only with no follow-up: 5-8% recovery

Part 1: The 8 message templates that work

Don't use the default. Use one of these instead, customized with your business name + first name.

Template 1: Service business standard

Hi, this is [name] at [biz]. Missed your call — text me what you need and I'll get back to you tomorrow morning. Reply 'urgent' if it can't wait.

Why it works: Plain "this is [name]" sounds human. Clear next step. Sets expectations on response time. Gives true emergencies a path.

Template 2: After-hours specific

Hey, [biz] is closed for the night — got your call though. Text me what you need and I'll respond by 9 AM. If it's an emergency reply 'urgent' and I'll call back tonight.

Why it works: Acknowledges the time. Specific response time reduces "I should call again later" hangup behavior.

Template 3: B2B / professional services

Hi, [name] from [biz] — I missed your call. Reply with what you need and I'll get back same business day. Or schedule directly: [your-cal-link].

Why it works: Professional tone. Self-serve scheduling option for prospects who want to skip the back-and-forth.

Template 4: Healthcare / appointment-based

Hi! [biz] here. We missed your call. Text us your name + reason for the call and we'll respond within 4 business hours. Existing patient? Reply 'patient'.

Why it works: HIPAA-aware (no health info expected in reply). Routes existing patients differently.

Template 5: High-volume retail

Hi, [biz]. Missed your call — text 'STORE' for hours, 'ORDER' to check on an order, or anything else for a callback in 1 business day.

Why it works: Self-serve options reduce back-and-forth on common questions.

Template 6: Solo operator

Hey, [name] here. Missed your call — heads down on a job. Text me what you need and I'll call back when I'm free. If it's an emergency reply 'now' and I'll step away.

Why it works: Honest about the constraint. Builds trust ("I'm working, not ignoring you").

Template 7: Multi-location

Hi, you reached [biz] [city]. We missed your call. Text us what you need + your zip code so we can route you to the closest team. Response within 1 hour during business hours.

Template 8: New customer focused

Hi! Got your call. New customer? Text 'NEW' and we'll send our most popular service options. Existing customer or other? Just text us what you need and we'll respond within an hour.

Why it works: Routes new prospects to a sales-friendly path.

Part 2: Setup by phone system

Google Voice (free)

  1. Open voice.google.com
  2. Click Settings (gear icon, top right)
  3. Click "Calls" in the left sidebar
  4. Find "Get a notification when you miss a call" — make sure it's on
  5. Find "Send text when you miss a call" — toggle on
  6. Paste your custom message
  7. Save

OpenPhone

  1. Open OpenPhone web app or mobile app
  2. Click Settings → "Auto-replies"
  3. Click "Add auto-reply" → "Missed call"
  4. Pick which numbers it applies to (usually all of yours)
  5. Paste your custom message
  6. Set the schedule (24/7, or "after hours only")
  7. Save

RingCentral

  1. Open RingCentral web app
  2. Click Settings → "Phone" → "Auto receptionist" or "After-hours rules"
  3. Find "Missed call SMS" or "Auto-text on missed call" (location varies by plan tier)
  4. Enable it
  5. Paste your custom message
  6. Save

Vonage Business / 8x8 / Dialpad / Nextiva / Ooma / Phone.com / Sideline

Look in Settings for any of these:

  • "Auto-reply on missed call"
  • "Missed call text"
  • "After-hours rules" → "Send SMS"
  • "Voicemail SMS notification" (note: this notifies YOU, not the caller — different setting)
If your provider truly doesn't have it, the cheapest fix is to forward your business number to a free Google Voice line and use Google Voice's auto-text instead.

Part 3: The 60-second response test

  1. Call your business number from your personal cell.
  2. Don't pick up.
  3. Wait 60-90 seconds.
  4. Read the auto-text on your personal cell.
  5. Verify the wording, response time, and that it actually fired.

Common failure modes

  • Auto-text didn't fire at all → setting wasn't saved correctly. Re-check.
  • Took more than 60 seconds → carrier-side delay; may be A2P registration issue.
  • Wrong message → editor field had stale content. Re-paste.
  • Message arrived but with weird formatting → emoji or special char in template; remove and retry.

Part 4: CRM integration & lead tagging

The auto-text by itself catches leads. The CRM tagging tells you whether it's working.

What to tag

Every lead recovered via missed-call text-back gets tagged in your CRM as recovered-from-missed-call. This lets you:

  1. Track close rate vs leads answered live (typical: 60-80% of live close rate).
  2. Calculate ROI — total revenue from this tag / cost of phone system feature.
  3. Identify when to upgrade to a Tier 1 AI receptionist.

How to wire it up

If you use Zapier: Trigger "New SMS from your VoIP" → Filter for SMS marked as auto-reply → Action: "Find or Create Contact in CRM" → Action: "Add Tag: recovered-from-missed-call".

If you use Make.com: Webhook from VoIP (or scheduled poll) → Filter contact texted in last 60s → Find or Create CRM contact → Add tag.

If you use HubSpot Free: HubSpot's email-to-contact feature can auto-create contacts from forwarded auto-reply emails. Manually tag for the first 30 days; then automate via workflow.

Upgrade when: you've recovered 4+ leads/week from free auto-text and want to push toward 80% capture rate.

AI holds full conversations and books callbacks. Multi-language. Deep CRM integration with conversation summaries.

Upgrade when: you've outgrown Tier 1 and have call volume justifying the spend.
