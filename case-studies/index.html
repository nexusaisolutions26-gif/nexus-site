Service Business · Landscaping

The problem

Quotes were the operational bottleneck. Owner was spending 12 minutes per quote drafting in Word, copying past quotes, hand-calculating labor and materials. With 6-12 quote requests a week, that was 90+ minutes of owner time on quote drafting alone — most done late at night, hurriedly. Quote quality suffered when rushed; close rate suffered when quotes went out 24+ hours after the inquiry.

What we built

  • A custom GPT trained on 30 of their past quotes, including specific pricing rules (labor markup, materials markup, minimum job, deposit terms).
  • Standard-format output: project summary, scope, three-tier pricing (Good/Better/Best), what's included/excluded, timeline, terms.
  • Plain-language voice tuned for residential homeowners — no contractor jargon.

Tools used

ChatGPT Plus ($20/mo) Custom GPT (free w/ Plus) Existing CRM (no change)

The result

Quote draft time dropped from 12 minutes to 90 seconds. Same-day quote turnaround on every inquiry. Close rate (quote-to-paid) lifted 18% over the next 90 days, attributed primarily to the speed-of-response advantage over slower competitors.

"Used to take me 12 minutes per quote and they all looked slightly different. Now I dictate the basics, the GPT drafts it in my voice, I send it. Saturday afternoon was usually quote-Saturday. Now Saturday is actually Saturday."
— Owner, Lawn Harmony Landscaping (Circleville, OH)
Custom Apparel · B2B

TagUp Tees

Custom apparel and merchandise for small businesses

3 days → 1.5 days

~6 hrs/wk

Operational savings

The problem

Order intake was manual — emails came in, were copy-pasted into a spreadsheet, then again into the production system. The ops manager spent 6+ hours a week just exporting and reconciling reports between three systems that didn't talk to each other.

What we built

  • A Make.com automation chain connecting their email intake, CRM, and production-tracking spreadsheet.
  • A custom GPT for drafting customer status emails ("your order is in production / shipped / delivered").
  • Auto-generated weekly ops reports pulled directly from the spreadsheet via Make + ChatGPT — replaced 6 hours of manual work with a 5-minute review.

Tools used

Make.com ($9/mo) ChatGPT Plus ($20/mo) Existing tools (Gmail, Sheets)

The result

Order-to-shipment time dropped from 3 days to 1.5 days because the manual handoff between intake and production was eliminated. The 6 hours per week the ops manager was spending on report exports went to actual customer service work. Combined with reduced overtime, operational savings worked out to roughly $2,400/month.

"We're a small custom apparel shop and were getting buried in spreadsheet work. They built us an automated workflow that cut our admin time in half. Tim was straightforward about what would and wouldn't work — appreciated the honesty."
— Operations Lead, TagUp Tees LLC

The problem

After-hours rental inquiries were dying in voicemail. Customers calling at 7pm to ask about availability rarely called back the next morning. The business was losing roughly 4-6 leads per week to this gap, each worth $300-1,200 in net new lease revenue.

What we built

  • An AI receptionist on a $40/month tier that answers after-hours calls, qualifies the inquiry (which appliance, urgency, location), and offers a callback time the next morning.
  • Auto-text fallback on missed calls — "Got your call, text us what you need and we will respond by 9 AM."
  • Structured call summaries auto-logged to their CRM so the team had context before calling back.

Tools used

AI receptionist ($40/mo) Existing phone line Existing CRM (Pipedrive)

The result

In the first 60 days the AI receptionist captured leads worth roughly $4,200 in net new lease revenue that would otherwise have died in voicemail. The math wasn't even close — $80 in tool cost recovered $4,200 in revenue.
